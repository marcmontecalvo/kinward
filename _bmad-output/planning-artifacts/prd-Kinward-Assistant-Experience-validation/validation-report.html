Kinward Assistant Experience — Validation Report

_bmad-output/planning-artifacts/prd-Kinward-Assistant-Experience.md
Fair

This PRD holds up well as a chain-top spec: its decision registers (§18–§19), per-requirement traceability (§17.4), and deterministic privacy/action policies (§8–§11) are genuinely strong and source-extractable for architecture and epic creation. The risks are concentrated in done-ness — several FRs encode thesis-level behavior behind unbounded words like "relevant" and "personality" — and in three user journeys that lack named protagonists, which will force story creators to infer context the PRD should carry inline. Resolve the vague FRs and name the floating UJ protagonists before marking the PRD final.

The adversarial pass materially sharpens the picture: it surfaced two direct contradictions (§14.1 vs PD-06 on credential material in backups; AD-01's interim behavior vs FR-002's "recoverable operation" at the same milestone), two defined-but-never-required concepts (the household fallback assistant has no existence FR; specialist-assistant delegation records have no enforcing FR), and an unmeasurable success-metrics frame ("sampled" is never given a sampling procedure). A separate exhaustive path walk found 72 unhandled edge cases, concentrated on lifecycle transitions the PRD never specifies: member removal and re-invitation, identity-state transitions on shared displays, indeterminate provider outcomes, and orphaned state after partial failures. The grade lands at Fair — not because the document is weak, but because one high-severity done-ness finding and the adversarial contradictions must be resolved before this PRD can safely feed UX, architecture, and stories.

Decision-readiness

strong

The PRD treats decisions as decisions. Sections 18 and 19 are genuine decision registers — not "considerations" or soft-balanced prose — with stable IDs (AD-01 through AD-15, PD-01 through PD-07), named owners, due milestones, affected-requirement lists, and a safe interim behavior on every row. The interim behaviors name what was given up, not just what was chosen. The readiness criteria (§20) are honest about open items; a decision-maker can act on this PRD.

The one structural gap is the absence of inline tension callouts: a story author reading FR-032 won't see inline that PD-02 is an unresolved blocker for that FR — they must cross-reference the register.

medium

Open-decision density at milestone gates

§18–§19
Twenty-two decisions are all "Open," several due at "Milestone A start" (AD-01, AD-02, AD-06) or "Milestone B start". AD-01 blocks "invitation, session, or recovery flows" and is due at Milestone A start, but FR-002 (bootstrap as "one recoverable operation") and FR-005 (invitation binding) are mapped to Milestones A and B. A story author may not realize Milestone A's bootstrap API cannot include session/recovery until AD-01 resolves.
Fix: Add inline [BLOCKED BY AD-01] / [BLOCKED BY PD-02] tags on the affected FRs so the blocker travels with the requirement, not only in the register.

Substance over theater

strong

No persona theater: nine user journeys (§5) each carry a protagonist and an outcome, and the traceability table (§17.4) proves each UJ drives specific FRs — no journey is decorative. No innovation theater: the thesis is encoded in §1's explicit "Kinward is not" list and the product outcomes (§2), not a novelty claim. No NFR theater: performance NFRs carry product-specific thresholds (NFR-017 "within 2 seconds at p95," NFR-020 "within 250 ms of the client receiving an identity downgrade"). No vision theater: §1 could not swap into another product's PRD. No findings — this dimension is clean.

Strategic coherence

strong

The PRD has a thesis: a private, continuous assistant relationship per person that infers household logistics from durable facts rather than requiring routine construction, with enforced privacy boundaries. FR-048 encodes the anti-routine thesis directly, tagged "Brief §Product Thesis" in traceability. The milestone arc (§16) and the proactive-level split in FR-045 sequence to serve the thesis rather than front-loading flashy features. Success metrics measure utility and cross-surface continuity, not DAU/MAU, and counter-metrics are present and thesis-aligned (§6.4, §6.5).

low

Thesis not labeled as thesis

§1, §2
The product thesis is implicit and only discoverable as "thesis" via the traceability source tag on FR-048. A story author who doesn't cross-reference the table may miss that FR-048 is load-bearing.
Fix: Add a one-sentence labeled thesis statement at the top of §1 or §2.

Done-ness clarity

adequate

Most FRs are testable (FR-001, FR-005, FR-076 have clear verifiable conditions) and the NFRs carry explicit bounds. But several FRs encode their core behavior behind words with no testable definition — "relevant exceptions" (FR-048), "household language" (FR-063), "personality and interaction preferences" (FR-012), "distinguish" (FR-022). The verification column in §17.4 names the method but not the acceptance condition; for a chain-top PRD that feeds story creation, the gap between "verification method" and "acceptance condition" is the main risk. The UJs partially compensate — §4.1.2's eight explicit completion conditions are exemplary — but that rigor is concentrated in the release-boundary sections, not the FR list.

high

"Relevant" is unbounded in thesis-level FRs

FR-048 §13.6, FR-063 §13.8
FR-048 ("detect relevant exceptions") and FR-063 ("translate relevant state and actions into household language") are load-bearing for the product thesis but neither defines what counts as "relevant," for whom, or by what test. A story author cannot write an acceptance test without inventing the definition.
Fix: Add a one-line definition per FR, e.g. for FR-048: "Relevant exception: a calendar, durable-fact, or integration-state change that affects a confirmed household fact, an adult's schedule, or a child's routine, as determined by the context-assembly rules in §12.2."
medium

"Personality and interaction preferences" undefined

FR-012 §13.2
Names no configurable dimensions, no persistence target, and no test. The verification is "integration" but what integration test proves a preference was configured?
Fix: Enumerate the configurable dimensions (e.g. name, tone, verbosity, proactivity default) or cross-reference a UX spec section that does.
medium

"Distinguish" has no observable surface

FR-022 §13.3
Doesn't say whether the distinction between confirmed facts, inferred observations, transient context, and household-shared facts is visible to the user, in the data model, or both. FR-043 partially covers the user-facing side, but FR-022's scope is ambiguous.
Fix: State whether "distinguish" means "store with a typed classification" (data model), "present visibly differently" (UI), or both.
low

FR-008 reviewable fields unspecified

§13.1
"Review and correct their profile information" doesn't list which fields.
Fix: Cross-reference the profile field list or state "at minimum: display name, household role label, and sharing defaults."

Adversarial review

review-adversarial-general.md

This PRD is unusually disciplined about privacy boundaries and deterministic policy states, which is exactly why the gaps stand out so sharply: the author lavishes precision on shared-surface identity transitions while leaving the actual measurability machinery, several defined-but-unbacked concepts, and a number of requirement/decision contradictions loose. At least a dozen requirements are untestable as written because their predicates ("relevant," "consequential," "sampled," "where supported") are undefined, and the milestone gate leans on at least two open architecture decisions whose interim behaviors directly contradict Milestone C FRs. A document this careful in places has no excuse for the holes below.

"Sampled" has no sampling procedure

§6.1, §17.4
"At least 70% of sampled assistant sessions," "at least 80% of sampled cross-surface topic continuations," and "at least 75% of sampled Now or briefing items" give no sample size, sampling method, selector, or evidence owner. An engineer cannot build a test that passes or fails these thresholds. The 30-day pilot window is stated, but the sampling frame inside it is not.

UJ-7's message-to-teacher flow has no backing FR and its channel is later scope

UJ-7, §4.3, §17.5
The journey says "Message sending is blocked or routed to authorized-adult approval," but §4.3 defers email to later scope and no FR defines any other message channel for a child. None of UJ-7's mapped requirements define a message-sending capability. The journey dodges the hard case by referencing a capability that does not exist in any milestone.

No FR requires the household fallback assistant to exist

§3.3, FR-011, FR-020, UJ-5
§3.3 defines it, FR-011 and FR-020 constrain it, UJ-5 depends on it — but no FR states that a deployment must actually create a fallback assistant, when, or who owns the lifecycle. FR-009 covers personal assistants; the fallback assistant has no equivalent existence requirement.

§14.1 and PD-06 contradict each other on credential material in backups

§14.1, PD-06, FR-074, FR-084
§14.1 lists "encrypted credential material where supported" as included backup data. PD-06's safe interim behavior says "Exclude credential material; every integration requires reauthorization after restore." A reviewer cannot tell whether encrypted credentials are in or out for Milestone C — the two sources give opposite answers.

FR-023's trigger "consequential, sensitive, or behavior-changing" is undefined

FR-023, §17.4
No section defines the threshold, so "require confirmation before becoming durable facts" is untestable — the same undefined-predicate problem as FR-048 and FR-063.

§8.2's escape clause makes FR-026 untestable while AD-07 is still open

FR-026, §8.2, AD-07
FR-026 (Milestone C, privacy-test) requires enforcement of §8.2, but §8.2 limits the invalidation obligation to dependencies "the product can identify," and AD-07 — which defines the invalidation/deletion mechanism — is open. The privacy-test gate has nothing concrete to test against.

Specialist assistants and delegation records have no enforcing FR

§3.2, §9.1, NFR-004
§9.1 specifies delegation records (purpose, permitted data, expiry, recipient) but no FR requires them to exist, be enforced, or be inspectable. NFR-004 names "specialist-assistant boundaries" as a test target with no FR backing the behavior under test.

"Shared display" (singular) vs two defined shared surface contexts

§4.1.1, §4.1.2, FR-035
The first live slice requires "shared display" but §4.1.1 defines shared kitchen and living-room displays. An engineer cannot determine whether satisfying one shared context satisfies the slice.

FR-042's subject "generated temporary views" is never defined in the PRD body

FR-042, §17.4
The term appears only in the traceability table; it has no definition, no journey, and no source beyond "UX §Generated Views." A requirement whose subject is undefined cannot be implemented or verified.

NFR-020 and §10 don't reconcile their timing models; both depend on open AD-10

NFR-020, §10, AD-10
§10's "next render" has no deadline bound to it, and the client-side 250 ms obligation presupposes a client notification mechanism AD-10 is supposed to define — yet NFR-020 is marked Milestone C with perf-test and privacy-test.

§6.2's 100% bar has no stated population

§6.2
"Zero actions are reported complete when the provider returned failure, timeout, unknown status, or no confirmation" — unclear whether "zero" applies across the 30-day pilot, all actions ever, or a sampled subset.

No user journey exercises the teen member

§7.3, §17.5
UJ-7 is explicitly a child. Teen-specific behaviors in §7.3 (money/transportation/appointment approval defaults, guardian-visible category disclosure) have no journey and no dedicated FR beyond the bundled FR-032.

AD-01's interim behavior collides with FR-002 at Milestone A

AD-01, FR-002
FR-002 requires bootstrap "as one recoverable operation" at Milestone A; AD-01 blocks recovery implementation until resolved and is due "Milestone A start." The PRD gives no rule for which prevails when an open AD contradicts a same-milestone FR.

§6.4's counter-metric is duplicated as NFR-027 and is not measurable

§6.4, NFR-027
A universal negative with no sampling or test procedure, owned twice (§6.4 and NFR-027) with no defined method to detect a violation during the pilot.

INV-1 and §4.4 rely on the undefined term "control-plane behavior"

INV-1, §4.4, §13.9
Given that the product includes Kinward Control, administrator management, and backup orchestration, the line between permitted household administration and forbidden "control-plane behavior" is unspecified and untestable as an invariant.

Edge-case walk (exploration lane)

review-edge-cases.md

An exhaustive path enumeration across all nine UJs, the role/privacy classes (§7), data-sharing boundaries (§8), feature areas, the backup/restore contract (§14), the NFRs, and both decision registers produced 72 unhandled paths (no severities by design — this lane reports gaps, not judgments). The densest clusters:

Lifecycle and orphaned state — partial bootstrap failure leaving an orphaned household record; invitation-token expiry mid-binding; re-invitation of a previously removed adult (prior private data restored, archived, or deleted?); household-shared topics whose owner is removed. Identity-state transitions — behavior between verified → likely → unknown on shared displays; backend-detects-but-client-unreachable partitions against NFR-020's dual deadline; private handoff when the personal device is offline. Indeterminate outcomes — provider returns timeout/unknown after approval (duplicate execution vs limbo); restart during an in-progress approved action; concurrent approvals mutating the same resource. Implicit members of fixed sets — role transitions the PRD only partially specifies (child→teen, teen→adult, adult→administrator handover), privacy-class transitions on existing content, and calendar events appearing in both private and household-shared calendars with conflicting details. Guardian gaps — a child profile with no configured guardian when a child-related briefing fires (silent drop or wrong-adult disclosure).

Drill into review-edge-cases.md for all 72 entries, each anchored to a PRD location with trigger, gap, and consequence. The file also lists the areas walked and found covered.

  • ID continuity is clean: FR-001–086, NFR-001–037, UJ-1–9, AD-01–15, PD-01–07, INV-1–11 — contiguous, no duplicates; §17.1 forbids renumbering.
