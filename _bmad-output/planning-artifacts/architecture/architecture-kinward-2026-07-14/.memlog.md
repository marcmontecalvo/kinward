- (decision) AD-01 amendment: account state is exactly active, disabled, locked, or recovery-pending; deletion-pending is a separate overriding person lifecycle. Only active without deletion-pending has authority. Entry into any non-active or deletion-pending condition atomically revokes sessions, grants, invitations, approvals, handoffs, delegations, provider access, and proactivity; cancels unsubmitted work; preserves submitted/unknown reconciliation blockers; deletion-pending permits reconciliation-only access; same-profile reactivation reauthenticates and revalidates all authority.
- (decision) AD-07 amendment: every protected resource uses one canonical metadata contract with owning principal type and UUID, data class, item version, versioned audience policy or grant reference, and derived lineage when applicable. Default denial and deny-overrides apply; current grant/policy revocation invalidates access and downstream use. Storage normalization is adapter-local.
- (decision) AD-12 amendment: backup obtains an application-coordinated mutation/worker barrier or transactionally consistent checkpoint at one commit boundary so action, outbox, activity, job, and deletion state cannot split; restore is validated whole-authority replacement rather than merge and activates all-or-nothing; unresolved attempts/jobs restore non-dispatchable until explicit reconciliation.
- (decision) AD-12 restore-authority amendment: direct household-authored content restores under joint current-administrator management; any current administrator may quarantine, one may dispose only without a conflicting same-version disposition, conflict requires all current administrators or an explicit versioned household policy, ambiguity remains quarantined, and last-administrator removal is rejected. Restored derived shares remain invalid and require new transformation; absent/deleted/non-reauthenticated owners grant administrators no inspection or release authority.
- (decision) AD-13 amendment: append protection applies to a sanitized immutable activity envelope and integrity chain; protected detail is a separately classified, separately authorized payload/view referenced by digest. Required deletion or person disposition erases or crypto-shreds protected payload and appends a disposition event while retaining only the permitted sanitized envelope/tombstone and minimum reconciliation state. New backups exclude erased payload; older archives restore it quarantined under AD-12.
- (decision) AD-20 amendment: every action has a canonical namespaced ActionTargetKey and conflict scope; prepared proposals may coexist but a database-enforced conflict lock permits at most one acting, submitted, unknown, or reconciling attempt per key. Transitions use expected-version compare-and-set and terminal states are immutable. Multi-principal approval uses one canonical object with all principal roles, policy/assignment/source versions, eligible decision makers, explicit any-one/all/threshold quorum, independently required affected-principal approvals, sharing references, expiry, serialized idempotent responses, fixed revocation/disablement then expiry then cancellation then decline then approval precedence, and atomic revalidation before acting.
- (decision) AD-21 amendment: within /api/v1 and the matching SSE major version, evolution is additive and backward-compatible. Older clients ignore unknown optional object fields and unknown non-terminal events safely, then query current state; unknown major versions, required enum values, and terminal semantics fail closed with upgrade-required behavior. Breaking changes require a new major API/event/schema version and cross-surface generated-client compatibility fixtures.
- (decision) AD-25 adopts the knowledge-state and external deletion lifecycle: knowledge state is confirmed durable fact, pending inferred observation, or transient context. Pending observations carry owner/subject, source identities and versions, class, confidence, and fixed 30-day expiry and are inspection-only; they cannot personalize, rank, trigger proactivity, authorize action, enter shared/fallback/provider context, or act as facts. Confirm creates a versioned durable fact; reject/delete/expiry removes the body, invalidates dependents, and suppresses recurrence from identical evidence. External deletion inability immediately blocks local and provider retrieval, marks the reference deletion-pending or externally-retained, submits provider deletion when exposed, and discloses the limitation to the owner. Binds facts, observations, providers, deletion, backup, and restore; prevents unconfirmed inference authority and externally retained data remaining usable.
